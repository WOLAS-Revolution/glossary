A

Accompaniment

A food item served with the main component of the meal to provide balance to the dish.

Aeration

Aeration is the process of incorporating air into a food item.

Alternate drop

Two or three different dishes are served alternatively around the table. Guests don't specifically order their dish, but are free to swap with their neighbour if they wish.

B

Baking

Baking is the process of heating food with hot air, but without the addition of oils.

See also

  • Oil

Ballotine

Traditionally, a ballotine is a deboned leg of poultry, stuffed, trussed and cooked. A ballotine is usually cooked by braising or poaching.

See also

  • Braising
  • Poaching
  • Poultry

Barding

The process of covering a food item in strips of fat while it cooks. The added fat helps keep the food moist, while imparting flavour.

See also

  • Fat

Basting

A technique where a food item is periodically coated with a sauce, marinade or its own juices during the cooking process. Basting during long cooking processes helps keep the meat moist.

See also

  • Sauce
  • Marinade

Batter

A semi-liquid mixture made by combining dry flours with wet ingredients, such as milk or eggs. The batter is often aerated and flavoured with the addition of other ingredients.

See also

  • Flour
  • Egg
  • Aeration

Bien cuit

Well done; thoroughly cooked

Biological contamination

Biological contamination occurs when food is infested with living microorganisms that are able to cause harm if ingested.

See also

  • Microorganism

Biscuit

A small crisp cake made of sweetened dough, rolled and sliced or dropped from a spoon and baked on a flat sheet.

See also

  • Cake
  • Dough

Blanching

To partially cook food in boiling water. This is often followed by refreshing.

See also

  • Refreshing

Blanquette

A French stew of veal, chicken, or lamb that has been blanched, then cooked in stock, thickened with egg yolks and cream, and garnished with mushrooms and pearl onions. The sauce is always white.

See also

  • Veal
  • Blanching
  • Sauce

Boning

See "Deboning".

See also

  • Deboning

Braise

A cut of meat that has been cooked by braising.

See also

  • Braising

Braising

In most cases, braising is performed on whole cuts of meat, but may also be used on woody vegetables and root vegetables. The ingredients in the braise are added to the cooking vessel, then half-covered with the cooking liquid and placed in an oven set to a low to moderate temperature.

Bread

Bread is a baked food, usually made from dough of flour and water. Most breads are aerated with yeast to make them soft and light.

See also

  • Dough
  • Flour
  • Aeration
  • Yeast

Brining

To soak protein-based foods in salted water. The food is usually soaked for 6-12 hours.

Brûlée

Refers to foods that are sprinkled with sugar and caramelized with a blowtorch, brûlée iron or salamander, forming a hard brittle top.

See also

  • Sugar

Brunoise

A kind of cut: Fine dice 2×2×2mm.

See also

  • Dice

Buffet

A selection of hot and cold food items from each course are made accessible to customers for a fixed fee.

C

Cake

A mixture usually made of flour, fat, eggs, sugar, liquid, and leavening. Some cakes are fried, but most are baked in an oven.

See also

  • Baking
  • Oven

Canapé

Canapés are a kind of appetiser. Canapés are defined by their hard base, such as a cracker, toasted bread, vol-au-vent or puff pastry. This base is then topped with a flavoursome spread and ingredients.

See also

  • Vol-au-vent

Carbohydrate

The body's primary fuel source, and are available as starch or sugar.

See also

  • Starch

Cartouche (action)

To cook foods en papillote, and to prevent loss of moisture during stewing and shallow poaching.

See also

  • Sugar

Cartouche (item)

A circular piece of greaseproof paper used to cover hot liquids as they cool to prevent a skin from forming.

Carving

Carving is the process of slicing and portioning a large cut of meat.

Caul

The lining around the organs in the internal cavity of most mammals. It can be used to wrap and shape meat-based farces.

See also

  • Farce

Chemical contamination

Contamination that occurs by food coming into contact with harmful chemicals or toxins.

See also

  • Chemicals
  • Toxins

Chemise

To line or coat a mould or an item with jelly or jam.

See also

  • Jelly
  • Jam

Chiffonade

A kind of cut: Shredded, 1mm thickness.

Choux paste

A French paste used for making many pastry products and desserts, including éclairs & cream puffs. Choux pastry is piped, rather than moulded into shape.

See also

  • Piping

Choux paste

A French paste used for making many pastry products and desserts, including éclairs & cream puffs. Choux pastry is piped, rather than moulded into shape.

See also

  • Piping
